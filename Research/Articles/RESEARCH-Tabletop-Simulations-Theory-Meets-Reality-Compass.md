## What It Is
A Compass Security blog post on the practical design and execution of cyber tabletop exercises, bridging the gap between theoretical incident response planning and how teams actually behave under realistic crisis conditions. Compass Security runs TTX engagements that simulate ransomware, data exfiltration, supply chain compromise, and insider threat scenarios, with a focus on stress-testing decision-making, escalation paths, and cross-functional coordination under pressure — not just technical response.

## Key Concepts
- A TTX is described as the cybersecurity equivalent of a fire drill — practise the response before the real incident forces costly lessons
- Effective exercises should: test decision-making under time pressure and incomplete information, validate roles and handoffs, expose communication and escalation gaps, and surface breakdowns between IT, security, legal, comms, and leadership
- Failure during the exercise is actively encouraged — finding gaps in a controlled setting is the point
- Role-specific, outcome-driven exercises are increasingly necessary given the cross-functional nature of modern incidents (executives, legal, regulators, customers all simultaneously affected)
- Key measurables: time to executive engagement, decision clarity, information flow, operational continuity in degraded mode, RTO/RPO under stress

## Relevance to Environment
- Directly relevant to the OT/SCADA assessment scope — a TTX simulating a ransomware or targeted attack on the fertilizer plant would exercise both IT/OT response and regulatory notification (CFIA, Explosives Act/ammonium nitrate)
